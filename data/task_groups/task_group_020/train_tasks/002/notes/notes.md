## English

This hidden notes file documents construction for `train_002`, the Project Meridian buyer-side SPA closing and economics package. It is not solver input. The source scenario is `SCN_020_ma_transaction_contract_review_and_negotiation`, drawing on the M&A agreement review patterns in examples `E001`, `E002`, and `E003`. The task follows the `train_002` design brief in `scratch/task_builder_briefs.md` and the closing-package family in `scratch/task_group_design.md`.

The shared environment is the M&A deal workbench in `task_group/task_group_020/env/`. The task uses generated public workbench records for `PRJ_MERIDIAN`: the deal row, `PB_BUYER_A` playbook rules, draft terms, cap table, consent inventory, employee rows, material contracts, regulatory row, diligence findings, benchmarks, documents, and notes. Environment source files, SQLite database files, manifests, seeds, and setup scripts are construction evidence only and are not copied into task payloads. The only solver-visible payload is `input/payloads/answer_template.json`.

The visible task asks buyer-side counsel for Verdantis Therapeutics plc to prepare a structured SPA closing/economics package for Meridian BioAnalytics. Solvers are expected to use `<TASK_ENV_BASE_URL>` web and API routes, optionally the read-only SQL endpoint, then return one JSON object. Key objects include `PRJ_MERIDIAN`, `PB_BUYER_A`, cap-table holders, consent IDs `CNS_PRJ_MERIDIAN_*`, employee IDs `EMP_PRJ_MERIDIAN_*`, material-contract IDs `MAT_PRJ_MERIDIAN_*`, finding `FND_PRJ_MERIDIAN_03`, and the regulatory row.

This task fits the group because it requires cross-system transaction-counsel synthesis rather than single-record lookup. The solver must combine economics, cap-table percentages, buyer playbook fallback positions, draft term deviations, consent and contract closing conditions, HSR status, employee liabilities, and operational readiness into one normalized legal work product. The data flow mirrors a real SPA closing package: finance data drives allocation and escrow math; legal terms and playbook rules drive indemnity and survival positions; diligence and contract systems drive closing conditions; regulatory and employment data drive readiness.

Material map: `prompt.txt` defines the role, target deal, accessible workbench APIs, output constraints, and required business sections. `answer_template.json` defines the schema, units, precision, stable-ID expectations, and controlled enum choices. `output/answer.json` is the standard answer. `eval/evaluate.py` contains deterministic checks for eight rubric points and converts raw weights to normalized score. `eval/eval.sh` is the required evaluator entry point.

Solution basis: headline value is 420,000,000 USD, upfront cash is 330,000,000 USD, stock value is 90,000,000 USD, and milestone value is zero. Holder allocation multiplies each cap-table fully diluted percentage by the cash, stock, and total consideration bases. The buyer playbook requires a 12.0% preferred indemnity cap, 10.0% fallback cap with identified-finding protection, 18-month preferred survival, and 15-month fallback survival if a 10.0% escrow is used. The standard answer therefore uses a 42,000,000 USD general escrow on purchase price with 15-month release, flags the seller's 6.0% cap, 12-month survival, and no materiality scrape as high risk, and requires a full breach-and-damages scrape. The working-capital collar amount is 2,520,000 USD from `FND_PRJ_MERIDIAN_03`. Required consent conditions are `CNS_PRJ_MERIDIAN_01` and `CNS_PRJ_MERIDIAN_03`; material-contract closing conditions are `MAT_PRJ_MERIDIAN_01` and `MAT_PRJ_MERIDIAN_03`; `CNS_PRJ_MERIDIAN_02` and `MAT_PRJ_MERIDIAN_02` are non-blocking notices. HSR is required, the approval is HSR only, and hell-or-high-water is not required. Employment treatment requires service credit for all three employee groups and PTO liability of 3,520,000 USD. Founder/executive restrictive covenants are required because the cap table flags management rollover and support obligations. D&O tail is treated as required for six years with cost borne by seller or purchase-price reduction; the workbench does not provide an amount. Overall readiness is `NOT_READY` with high risk.

Rubric and evaluation basis: there are eight all-or-nothing scoring points with raw weights `[3, 2, 2, 3, 2, 2, 1, 3]`, total raw weight 18. They cover at least eight distinct business outcomes: holder allocation, indemnity/escrow, NWC mechanics, consents and material contracts, regulatory status, employee and restrictive covenants, D&O and expenses, and closing readiness. Each point checks normalized IDs, enums, booleans, integer dollars, and declared percentage precision. No point uses subjective prose. Likely pitfalls include allocating only cash instead of total consideration, using upfront cash instead of headline value for percentages, missing `MAT_PRJ_MERIDIAN_03` because it appears in material contracts rather than consents, treating HSR as not required despite the regulatory row, omitting PTO totals, and marking the deal ready while blockers remain.

Transfer design: as a train task, this teaches solvers the group convention for closing-package tasks. They should learn to inspect multiple deal endpoints, use playbook fallback rules when draft terms are below buyer policy, calculate consideration by cap-table percentages, separate true closing blockers from post-closing notices, and use stable IDs in normalized JSON. The task also reinforces that missing workbench amounts can be represented through controlled statuses rather than invented dollars, and that readiness is a business classification derived from multiple systems.

本任务符合任务组主题，因为它要求跨系统完成交易律师综合判断，而不是查找单一记录。求解器必须把经济数据、股本比例、买方 playbook fallback、草案条款偏差、同意和合同交割条件、HSR 状态、员工负债和运营 readiness 合并成规范化法律工作成果。其数据流模拟真实 SPA 交割工作包：财务数据驱动分配和 escrow 计算；法律条款和 playbook 规则驱动赔偿和 survival 立场；尽调和合同系统驱动交割条件；监管和员工数据驱动 readiness 判断。
