### Exact hidden solution and calculation basis

No task-local adjustment was needed. The specified cohort is populated, all eligible cases have effective history at the cutoff, the top-three ordering is unambiguous under the requested tie-break, and the median and classification are non-degenerate.

Construction used these exact rules over the retained baseline:

1. Select `support_cases` whose `opened_at` is within the inclusive request window after joining `accounts`, requiring `segment='ENTERPRISE'`, `region IN ('NORTH','EAST')`, `is_internal=0`, and `is_test=0`. This yields 159 stable case IDs across 60 accounts.
2. Deduplicate `case_events` on `(source_system, external_event_id)`, retaining greatest `(ingested_at, case_event_id)`. The whole table has 28,000 imported rows, 26,800 effective rows, and 1,200 retry copies. Eligible cases have 729 imported rows, 704 effective rows, and 25 retry copies.
3. Retain effective events with `event_at <= '2026-05-31T23:59:59Z'`. Within each case, order by `(event_at, case_event_id)`. A case whose last event is `RESOLVED` is resolved; every other last event is active. A last `REOPENED` event identifies the reopened subset. Thus the latest-event distribution is 108 `RESOLVED`, 33 `OPEN`, 10 `CUSTOMER_REPLIED`, 7 `ESCALATED`, and 1 `REOPENED`. The active total is 51 and includes the reopened case; the reopened subset is 1.
4. The first-response target is the earliest effective `AGENT_RESPONDED` event, or the cutoff when none exists. The resolution target is the last effective `RESOLVED` event when resolved at cutoff, or the cutoff when active. For either clock, active seconds equal target time minus `support_cases.opened_at` minus all closed customer-wait intervals ending by that target. Each `WAITING_CUSTOMER` is paired to its first later `CUSTOMER_REPLIED` under stable event ordering. An interval is subtracted only when its reply closes by the relevant target. There are 16 eligible cases with customer-wait activity and no agent-response event.
5. Compare unrounded active hours strictly greater than the threshold for the case priority. Missing first responses use active elapsed time at cutoff. Active cases use active elapsed resolution time at cutoff. The result is 123 first-response breaches and 89 active-clock resolution breaches.
6. A severe active case is active at cutoff, priority `URGENT` or `HIGH`, and beyond its resolution active-time threshold. There are 24, sorted as:

   `CASE-000460`, `CASE-000775`, `CASE-002404`, `CASE-002836`, `CASE-002900`, `CASE-003268`, `CASE-003444`, `CASE-003519`, `CASE-003555`, `CASE-003700`, `CASE-003800`, `CASE-003875`, `CASE-003928`, `CASE-004104`, `CASE-004360`, `CASE-004419`, `CASE-004664`, `CASE-004675`, `CASE-004739`, `CASE-004920`, `CASE-005324`, `CASE-005335`, `CASE-005580`, `CASE-005639`.

7. Aggregate the severe active count and all resolution active-clock breach count by account. Sort severe count descending, breach count descending, and stable `account_id` ascending. The first three are:

   - `ACC-0210`: 2 severe active cases and 2 active-clock breaches.
   - `ACC-0315`: 2 severe active cases and 2 active-clock breaches.

8. The 108 cases resolved at cutoff have active resolution durations sorted before rounding. The 54th and 55th values are both `24.683333333333334` hours, so their average is the same and the requested final two-decimal median is `24.68`.

The standard answer is therefore: eligible 159; active/open 51; reopened subset 1; response breaches 123; active-clock resolution breaches 89; the three account objects above; the exact 24-case severe set; median 24.68 hours; and risk `SEVERE`. Counts use stable business IDs rather than joined event-row counts, and only the final median is rounded.

### Output contract and atomic evaluation

The output has eight required business outcomes. Objects reject extra fields, the account ranking has exactly three objects in the declared order, severe IDs are unique and ascending, counts are JSON integers, the median is a finite JSON number at two-decimal precision, and risk is one of `CONTROLLED`, `ELEVATED`, or `SEVERE`.

The evaluator has exactly eight non-fractional points with raw total weight 18:

- `SP001`, weight 2: `eligible_case_count` must be the exact eligible population.
- `SP002`, weight 3: both `case_state_summary.open_at_cutoff_count` and `reopened_at_cutoff_count` must match as one complete state outcome.
- `SP003`, weight 2: `first_response_breach_count` must match.
- `SP004`, weight 3: `active_clock_resolution_breach_count` must match.
- `SP005`, weight 2: every required field and position in the three-object `worst_accounts` ranking must match.
- `SP006`, weight 3: the complete sorted `severe_active_case_ids` output must match.
- `SP007`, weight 2: `median_active_resolution_hours` must match the two-decimal standard.
- `SP008`, weight 1: `support_risk` must match the policy enum.

Each point earns exactly `weight / 18` or zero; there is no within-point fractional credit. The points cover population, case state, response SLA, resolution SLA, account prioritization, severe-case identification, duration distribution, and risk judgment as distinct outcomes. An unreadable or non-object candidate fails all eight without a traceback. A wrong ordinary business value affects only the point that owns that result. The standard answer self-scores 1.0. Construction sensitivity checks changed one numeric population value, one independently scored ranking value, and the risk enum; they failed only `SP001`, `SP005`, and `SP008`, respectively.

Likely solver pitfalls are counting imported event rows instead of cases; ignoring retry copies; trusting `support_cases.current_status`; using ingestion time as event time; including internal/test accounts; treating `CUSTOMER_REPLIED` or `ESCALATED` as closed; overlooking an effective reopen; failing to remove closed customer-wait time; treating a missing response as non-breached; using `>=` rather than strict `>`; ending active resolution clocks before the cutoff; ranking on rounded or incomplete rollups; using account names instead of stable IDs; taking the median before filtering to resolved-at-cutoff cases; rounding clock values before comparison; or applying the risk thresholds independently instead of as paired conditions.

The train transfer signal is schema-led exploration, source/external event deduplication, event-derived cutoff state, production exclusions, closed-wait interval accounting, response and resolution clock targets, reopen handling, stable business-ID counting, deterministic account ranking, even-population median convention, final-only rounding, and paired risk-threshold judgment. Comparing an attempted answer with the standard and point-level evaluator results can reveal which of those habits failed without turning the solver-visible request into a tutorial. ### 隐藏解法与精确计算

无需调整任务范围：指定群体非空、所有合格工单在截止时刻都有有效历史、前三账户排序明确，中位数和风险枚举也不退化。

先连接 `support_cases` 和 `accounts`，要求 `segment='ENTERPRISE'`、区域为 `NORTH` 或 `EAST`、`is_internal=0`、`is_test=0`，并按含端点开单时间过滤，得到 60 个账户的 159 个稳定工单。`case_events` 按 `(source_system, external_event_id)` 去重，保留 `(ingested_at, case_event_id)` 最大行。全表 28,000 行中有 26,800 个有效事件和 1,200 个重试副本；本群体 729 行中有 704 个有效事件和 25 个副本。

只使用截止时刻及之前的有效事件，并按 `(event_at, case_event_id)` 确定每个工单最后事件。最后事件为 `RESOLVED` 的 108 个工单视为已解决，其余均为活动工单；最后事件为 `REOPENED` 的工单构成重开子集。最后事件分布为 108 个 `RESOLVED`、33 个 `OPEN`、10 个 `CUSTOMER_REPLIED`、7 个 `ESCALATED` 和 1 个 `REOPENED`，因此活动/开放总数为 51，重开子集为 1。

首次响应目标是最早的有效 `AGENT_RESPONDED`，缺失时使用截止时刻；解决目标对截止时刻已解决工单使用最后解决事件，对活动工单使用截止时刻。活动秒数等于目标减 `support_cases.opened_at`，再减去目标前已由后续首个 `CUSTOMER_REPLIED` 闭合的 `WAITING_CUSTOMER` 区间。共有 16 个合格工单含客户等待活动且没有代理响应事件。按优先级使用严格大于阈值判断，得到 123 个首次响应违约和 89 个解决活动时钟违约。

活动且优先级为 `URGENT`/`HIGH`、同时超过解决活动时钟阈值的严重工单共有 24 个，其完整升序列表与 `answer.json` 一致。按严重活动工单数降序、全部解决时钟违约数降序、账户 ID 升序排序，前三名依次为：`ACC-0210`（2，2）、`ACC-0315`（2，2）、`ACC-0125`（1，3）。

108 个已解决工单按未舍入活动解决小时排序，第 54 和第 55 个值都为 `24.683333333333334`，最终两位小数中位数为 `24.68`。严重活动率为 `24/159=0.1509433962`，首次响应违约率为 `123/159=0.7735849057`；两组比例不满足受控或提升状态的成对严格阈值，因此风险为 `SEVERE`。计数均使用稳定业务 ID，只对最终中位数舍入。
